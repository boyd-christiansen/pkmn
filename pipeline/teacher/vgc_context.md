## How a champion reasons each turn

1. **Endgame Win-Condition Mapping**
   *Question:* What is my exact, mathematical path to victory from this board state, and which specific opposing threat absolutely prevents it?
   *Why:* Anchoring every micro-decision to the ultimate endgame prevents sacrificing the exact piece needed to close out the match.
2. **Speed Tier & Turn Order Deduction**
   *Question:* Factoring in active speed control, priority brackets, and dynamic mid-turn modifiers, what is the exact execution order this turn?
   *Why:* Miscalculating speed tiers leads to losing key offensive pieces before they can act, instantly throwing away momentum.
3. **Worst-Case Scenario Mitigation**
   *Question:* What is the opponent's most devastating potential play (e.g., Fake Out, redirection, double-target), and does my intended line survive it?
   *Why:* High-level play prioritizes risk mitigation; covering the opponent's optimal or most punishing branch prevents immediate, unrecoverable losses.
4. **Irreversible Resource Commitment**
   *Question:* Does committing my Terastallization or limited field condition right now secure an insurmountable advantage, or does it leave my endgame exposed?
   *Why:* Burning high-value defensive or offensive resources too early trades long-term flexibility for short-term survival.
5. **Positioning & Action Economy**
   *Question:* Can I achieve my goal through a safe pivot, a calculated sacrifice, or a 100% accurate attack rather than relying on a high-variance prediction?
   *Why:* Clean positioning maintains tempo without relying on 50/50 guessing games or RNG.

## Durable principles

* **The Clean Switch (Sacrificial Fodder):** Allowing a spent or low-HP Pokemon to faint naturally is superior to hard-switching, as it guarantees a fragile sweeper enters the field undamaged.
* **Stalling Temporary Power Spikes:** When the opponent activates Tailwind, Trick Room, or weather, prioritize Protect and defensive pivoting to waste their limited turns rather than trading damage.
* **Playing to Your Outs:** When ahead, minimize variance with 100% accurate, safe plays; when mathematically lost, embrace high-risk, low-probability sequences as your only path to victory.
* **Redirection Bypass:** Spread moves completely ignore redirection (Follow Me/Rage Powder), allowing you to bypass defensive decoys and damage the primary threat.
* **Punishing Passive Play:** If an opponent is highly incentivized to Protect or stall, exploit the free turn to set up, pivot, or double-target their partner.
* **Dynamic Mid-Turn Speed:** Speed recalculates instantly mid-turn; manually overriding weather or using priority speed control immediately shifts the turn order before attacks resolve.
* **Intimidate & Stat-Drop Deterrence:** Bringing Defiant/Competitive users or utilizing Clear Amulet turns the opponent's passive debuff cycling into an immediate offensive liability.
* **Endgame Simplification:** Focus all early-game resources on isolating and eliminating the opponent's sole counter to your late-game win condition.
* **The Opportunity Cost of Protect:** While Protect preserves HP, it concedes board momentum; use it actively to stall field conditions or scout, not just to delay an inevitable KO.
* **Information Asymmetry:** Hide your tech and deduce theirs via ability activation order and damage output; perfect information eliminates variance and dictates safe plays.

## Strategic roles & archetypes

* **Speed Control Setter:** Dictates the fundamental turn order via Tailwind, Trick Room, or Icy Wind, allowing slower, high-damage partners to strike first and sweep before taking damage.
* **Redirection Support:** Draws single-target attacks and status away from fragile setup sweepers using Follow Me or Rage Powder, effectively trading its own HP to guarantee a partner's win condition.
* **Intimidate / Stat-Debuff Pivot:** Cycles in and out of the field to systematically lower opposing offensive stats, mitigating damage and creating safe entry windows for allies.
* **Weather / Terrain Setter:** Automatically alters the global field state upon entry to boost specific move types, enable speed-doubling abilities, or block priority and status.
* **Fake Out / Priority Disruptor:** Secures immediate turn-one board control by flinching or taunting a key threat, buying a free turn to establish speed control or set up.
* **Wide Guard Support:** Completely neutralizes powerful spread-damage win conditions, protecting vulnerable partners and forcing the opponent into predictable single-target attacks.
* **Setup Sweeper:** Trades a turn of vulnerability for game-ending offensive boosts, forcing the opponent to commit multiple resources to stop them before they snowball.
* **Late-Game Cleaner:** A fast or priority-wielding offensive threat kept hidden and healthy in reserve to sweep the remaining board once opposing speed control and defensive walls expire.
* **Priority Blocker:** Passively shuts down opposing priority moves and Fake Out via abilities or terrain, protecting frail sweepers and ensuring your speed control dictates the turn order.
* **Trapper / Lock-In Disruptor:** Prevents the opponent from switching via Shadow Tag or locks them into passive moves via Encore, isolating favorable matchups and neutralizing defensive pivoting.
